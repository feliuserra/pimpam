# PimPam Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

PimPam is built on the belief that social networks are harmful because of how they're built and who they're built for — not because social connection itself is harmful. We're removing the specific structural incentives that cause most of the damage — algorithmic amplification, surveillance capitalism, centralised control — and seeing what grows in their place. The way we treat each other in this community is part of that experiment.

PimPam is built for everyone. This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the PimPam project — the codebase, issue trackers, discussion forums, community channels, and any other space where people interact under the PimPam name.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and respectful environment for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This isn't a formality. We mean it.

## Our standards

### Behavior we encourage

- Treating every person with kindness and respect.
- Being empathetic and patient, especially with people who are new or learning.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully.
- Taking responsibility when you make mistakes and learning from them.
- Focusing on what's best for the community, not just yourself.
- Respecting different perspectives, experiences, and opinions.
- Using welcoming and inclusive language.

### Behavior we don't tolerate

- Harassment of any kind, including unwanted sexual attention, advances, or comments.
- Trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks.
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person).
- Publishing someone's private information without their explicit consent (doxxing).
- Sustained disruption of discussions, events, or collaborative work.
- Discrimination based on any protected characteristic.
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behavior.
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

### A note on disagreement

Disagreement is healthy and welcome. You can disagree with an idea, a design choice, or a technical approach — strongly, even passionately. What you cannot do is attack the person behind the idea. Critique the work, not the human.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces. It also applies when a person is officially representing the project in public spaces, such as using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by emailing **conduct@pimpam.org** (to be established). All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

You can also report by opening a private issue if you're more comfortable with that.

We will never retaliate against anyone who reports a violation in good faith.

### How we respond

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct. Responses to violations will be proportionate and may include:

1. **Private conversation.** A maintainer reaches out to discuss the behavior and clarify expectations. Most situations are resolved here.

2. **Public correction.** A brief, public statement clarifying the violated standard. No personal details about the reporter are shared.

3. **Temporary suspension.** The person is temporarily removed from project spaces. Duration depends on the severity and nature of the violation.

4. **Permanent ban.** For severe or repeated violations, the person is permanently removed from all project spaces.

### Accountability for maintainers

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences as determined by other maintainers or the broader project governance. Nobody is above these standards.

## For the platform itself

This Code of Conduct governs the development community. The PimPam platform itself will have its own community guidelines and moderation policies, designed collaboratively and rooted in the same values of respect, safety, and inclusion.

Community moderation on the platform will be transparent, community-driven, and will prioritize the safety of vulnerable people.
